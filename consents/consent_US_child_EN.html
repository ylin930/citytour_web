Study Information and Statement of Informed Consent

Thank you for your interest in participating in our study on memory development. Please take your time to read this text carefully.

1. Aim of the Study

This study is a research project of the Max Planck Institute (MPI) for Human Development, Berlin, Germany. The purpose of this study is to investigate the development of memory capacities across childhood and in adulthood.

2. Procedure and Content of the Study

Your child will be asked to complete a simple task that involves watching videos, each providing a guided tour of a different magical city. The study consists of three sessions, scheduled 48 to 72 hours (2–3 days) after the previous session.

  • In each session, your child will watch a short city tour video lasting about six minutes and then answer questions about the places they saw.
  • Each tour will be followed by three sets of questions, with 12 questions in each set (36 in total per tour).
  • At the end of the third session, your child will answer an additional 84 questions about the two cities visited earlier.
  • Each session is expected to take approximately 45 minutes.
  • The study should be completed using a laptop computer with a reliable internet connection (no mobile phones or tablets).
  • Children are expected to answer the questions independently, without assistance from family or friends. Parents may provide help only with technical aspects such as mouse clicks and volume/screen brightness adjustment.

3. What Will Happen to the Data Collected?

The data collected will be used for research purposes only. The study data will be stored under an individual code number. Upon completion of data collection, the individual anonymized code linking the contact data to the task data will be deleted, making it impossible to link the study data to you in the future.

The study data may be shared with cooperation partners for collaborative analysis. The study data may also be made publicly accessible via research databases (Open Science Framework) or scientific publications (typically via the Internet). This allows other researchers to check or replicate the results and enhances the quality of scientific research. The study data may also be used for new research questions going beyond the purposes of this particular study. Study data are only transferred or made publicly accessible without individualized IDs or any data in which persons are identifiable.

4. Risks and Compensation for Taking Part in the Study

There are no known risks involved.

This study consists of three sessions, each lasting 45 minutes, for a total of approximately 2 hours and 15 minutes. Your child will be compensated at a rate of $13 per hour, with an additional $5.75 bonus for completing all three sessions, totaling $35.

Each session must be scheduled 48 to 72 hours (2–3 days) after the previous one. Please note that you and your child may withdraw from the study at any time. Failure to complete a task within 48–72 hours after the previous session will also be considered a withdrawal from the study. In such cases, your child will receive only partial payment corresponding to the time they participated:

  • If your child completes session 1 only: $10 (rounded up from $9.75).
  • If your child completes sessions 1 and 2: $20 (rounded up from $19.50).
  • If your child quits mid-session, payment will be proportional to completed time in 30-minute increments.

At the end of the study, you will receive a virtual Amazon gift card sent to your email with the amount depending on partial or complete participation.

5. Legal Basis

The legal basis for the processing of your data is your consent, in accordance with Article 6(1)(a) of the GDPR. You may withdraw your consent at any time with effect for the future. You may also withdraw your consent to the processing and use of your data at any time without negative consequences. Please note that once the code list has been deleted, it is no longer possible to link your browser-based task entrance code to your study data.

If you have any questions or concerns, please contact: Dr. Ying Lin (raven@mpib-berlin.mpg.de).

6. Data Subject Rights

You have the following rights with regard to your child’s personal data:

  • Right of access (Art. 15 GDPR)
  • Right of rectification (Art. 16 GDPR)
  • Right to erasure (Art. 17 GDPR)
  • Right to data portability (Art. 20 GDPR)
  • Right to restriction of processing (Art. 18 GDPR)
  • Right to object to data processing or use (Art. 21 GDPR)

You may also lodge a complaint with the competent data protection authority if you believe your data have been processed unlawfully. The competent authority for the Max Planck Society is the Bavarian Data Protection Authority, P.O. Box 606, 91511 Ansbach, Germany.

7. Participation Is Voluntary

Participation in this study is voluntary. You may withdraw from the study at any time without giving any reason and without any negative consequences. You may also withdraw your consent to data processing and usage at any time with effect for the future and without negative consequences. To do this, please contact Dr. Ying Lin at raven@mpib-berlin.mpg.de. Since no personally identifiable information is collected, it will not be possible to link your study data to you; hence, it will not be possible to withdraw your consent retroactively.
