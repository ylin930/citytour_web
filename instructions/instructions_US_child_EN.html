Dear Parents,

Please carefully read the following instructions. Your cooperation is essential for advancing the science of memory development.

Before beginning the study, please:

  • Maximize the browser window to full screen.
  • Ensure your child is seated comfortably and facing the computer screen.
  • Set your computer brightness to a comfortable level (brighter is better).
  • Adjust the volume to a comfortable level (there will be audio in the animations).
  • Make sure the environment is quiet and free from distractions for the entire session (about 45 minutes).

Study Procedure:

  • First, your child will watch an animation for approximately 6 minutes.
  • Afterwards, they will answer questions about what they saw. Each question will show three answer choices, displayed from left to right at the bottom of the screen.
  • Use the mouse to click on the image that corresponds to your child’s choice.

Important:

  • Do not assist your child in answering any of the questions. It is expected that they may encounter new words or unfamiliar content in the animations. Please do not explain, label, or influence their attention in any way.
  • The key goal of our research is that your child watches attentively and answers all questions independently.
  • You should stop the study at any time if your child feels uncomfortable, distressed, or wishes to discontinue.
  • Your child can take a break (up to 5 minutes) between the subtasks within each session (3 breaks in sessions 1 and 2, and 5 in session 3).

Important Note: Exiting full screen during the session or not completing the study within 48–72 hours after the previous session will be considered a withdrawal from the experiment.

Thank you very much for your support! Your participation is essential to the progress of our research on memory development.
